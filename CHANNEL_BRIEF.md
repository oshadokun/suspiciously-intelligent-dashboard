# Channel Brief — Suspiciously Intelligent

> Reference this file whenever you are generating content ideas, writing titles, hooks, captions, or evaluating whether a story fits the channel.

---

## Channel Identity

**Name:** Suspiciously Intelligent
**Format:** AI-focused media channel — YouTube primary, cross-posted to Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, X
**Positioning:** Explains AI news, tools, risks, culture, business moves, and strange developments in a way that is accessible but genuinely intelligent.

The channel sits between:
- Too technical (academic papers, developer content)
- Too shallow (mainstream "AI will take your job" panic content)

The audience is smart, curious, and slightly sceptical of hype — in both directions. They distrust obvious doom-saying as much as they distrust corporate cheerleading.

---

## Tone — What It Is

| Quality | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Smart** | Assumes the audience can follow a real argument. No hand-holding. No over-explaining. |
| **Observant** | Notices things others miss — patterns, contradictions, buried details in press releases |
| **Slightly suspicious** | Questions motives, funding, timing, and framing. Not conspiratorial. Just not naive. |
| **Clear** | One idea per sentence. Plain language for complex ideas. No jargon without explanation. |
| **Dry** | Wit comes from observation, not performance. The joke is in what you notice, not how you deliver it. |

---

## Tone — What It Is NOT

Reject any content idea or framing that leans into these:

- **Not panicked.** "AI is going to destroy everything" is not this channel.
- **Not cheerleading.** "This changes everything forever" is not this channel.
- **Not corporate.** No press release language. No "exciting partnership" framing.
- **Not childish.** No "mind-blowing," "insane," "you won't believe."
- **Not conspiratorial.** Sceptical of claims ≠ assuming bad faith by default.
- **Not generic.** Every video must be about something specific happening right now.

---

## Audience Profile

**Primary:** 25–45 year olds who work in or adjacent to tech, finance, media, or knowledge work. They use AI tools daily. They want to understand what is actually happening, not be told how to feel about it.

**Secondary:** Curious non-technical professionals who have noticed AI is changing their world and want reliable signal over noise.

**They follow this channel because:**
- It respects their intelligence
- It doesn't hype or fearmonger
- It catches things other channels miss
- The framing feels honest, not performed

---

## Content Rules

### Titles
- Under 65 characters
- Sentence case — never ALL CAPS, never Title Case Every Word
- Must contain a specific claim or question — not a vague theme
- Good: `"OpenAI just changed how it makes decisions. Here's why that's odd."`
- Bad: `"The SHOCKING truth about AI in 2025"`
- Bad: `"Everything You Need To Know About ChatGPT"`

### Hooks
- First sentence must create genuine intrigue — not panic, not hype
- Should feel like something a smart person says at the start of a conversation
- Good: `"There's a detail buried in this announcement that nobody's talking about."`
- Bad: `"What I'm about to show you will change how you think about AI FOREVER."`

### Frames that work well for this channel
- "Here's what the press release didn't say"
- "This looks like X but is actually Y"
- "Everyone's focused on [obvious thing]. The real story is [overlooked thing]."
- "This company is doing something unusual and nobody's asked why yet."
- "Two things happened this week that are connected. Here's how."

### Frames to avoid
- "Top 10 AI tools you NEED"
- "AI is coming for [profession]"
- "The AI revolution is here"
- Anything that reads like it was written by a marketing department

---

## Story Fit Criteria

A story fits this channel if it has at least one of:

1. **Something unexpected** — a company doing something that contradicts its public positioning
2. **An underreported angle** — the real story is buried under the obvious headline
3. **A genuine public concern** — something people are confused or worried about that deserves a clear-headed explanation
4. **A pattern** — connecting two or more events that reveal something about the industry
5. **A question nobody's asking** — the channel asks it first, clearly

A story does NOT fit if:
- It is a straight product announcement with no unusual angle
- It is pure speculation with no evidence
- It requires the audience to be scared to engage with it
- It has already been covered exhaustively by every major outlet with nothing new to add

## Platform-Specific Notes

### YouTube (long form)
- 8–18 minutes is the sweet spot
- Needs a clear argument, not just a summary of news
- Should leave the audience thinking differently about something

### YouTube Shorts / Instagram Reels / TikTok
- 30–75 seconds
- One idea only. No sub-points.
- Hook in the first 2 seconds or it fails
- Visual concept matters — what does the viewer SEE?

### LinkedIn
- More analytical framing — "here's what this means for business"
- Longer captions acceptable
- Avoid the inspirational-post format entirely

### X / Twitter
- Punchy. One observation per thread item.
- The first tweet must work standalone
- Good for "here's the thing nobody mentioned" takes

### Instagram image / carousel
- Carousel format: first slide is a hook, last slide is a takeaway or CTA
- Visual direction matters — clean, minimal, text-driven, not stock-photo-heavy
- Caption should add something the slides don't say

### Community posts (YouTube)
- Questions that don't have an obvious answer
- Polls where both options are defensible
- Avoid "which AI tool do you use?" generic polls

---

## Red Lines

Never recommend content that:
- Makes specific financial predictions
- Claims insider knowledge the channel does not have
- Presents speculation as fact
- Could be read as targeting or harassing an individual
- Sensationalises harm or danger beyond what the evidence supports
